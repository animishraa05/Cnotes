### Revised 4-Day Workout Plan: Compound-Heavy for Faster Aesthetic Gains


**Quick Updates**:
- **Frequency**: Still 4 days (e.g., Mon/Thu: Upper; Tue/Fri: Lower; rest Wed/Sat/Sun for studies/recovery).
- **Sets & Reps**: Compounds: 3-4 sets x 6-10 reps (focus on heavy, controlled—builds dense muscle fast). Isolations: 2 sets x 10-15 reps (pump for shape). Rest 2-3 min compounds, 90 sec isolations. Hit 8-10 reps on heavies before adding weight.
- **Progression**: Same as before—load/reps weekly. Warm-up/cool-down unchanged.
- **Time**: 40-50 min/session—efficient for CSE grind.
- **Why This Ratio?**: Compounds recruit more fibers (EMG data shows 2-3x activation), spiking testosterone/GH for aesthetics. Less iso keeps it sustainable.

### **Upper Body Days (Mon & Thu)**
Prioritizing pushes/pulls for broad chest/back/shoulders. Compounds dominate for that "big but lean" look.

   - Sets/Reps: 4 x 6-8  
   - Tutorial: [Jeff Nippard Rows](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3pT4Qtdcsdc) – Hinge, pull to ribs.

3. **Overhead Press** (Shoulders/Triceps) – Compound  
   - Sets/Reps: 3 x 6-10  
   - Tutorial: [Jeff Nippard OHP](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_RlRDWO2jfg) – Press overhead, core tight.

4. **Pull-Ups (Wide Grip)** (Back/Biceps) – Compound  
   - Sets/Reps: 3 x 6-10 (assisted if needed)  
   - Tutorial: [Jeff Nippard Pull-Ups](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Hdc7Mw6BIEE) – Chin over bar, full range.

5. **Lateral Raises** (Shoulders – Isolation Finisher)  
   - Sets/Reps: 2 x 10-15  
   - Why: Caps delts for width (20% iso slot).  
   - Tutorial: [Jeff Nippard Lateral Raises](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8iPEnn-ltC8) – Slight bend, raise to shoulder height.

**Total Compounds**: 80% volume. End with 2-min plank if time.

#### 1. **Lemon-Ginger Zinger Shake** (Citrusy Sour, Extra Refreshing – ~18g Protein, 120 Cal)

Lemon's acidity mimics dahi's tang but thins it more (watery base). Zesty for focus—citrus can perk you up like coffee without jitters.

**Ingredients** (Scaled for 60-70g Soya):

- 60-70g dry soya chunks (boiled & squeezed as before)
- Juice of 2-3 lemons (for sharp sour—adjust to taste)
- 300-400ml water (or coconut water for electrolyte boost)
- 1-inch ginger (grated) + 2-3 green chilies (for heat)
- 1 tsp jeera powder + pinch black salt/chaat masala
- Handful mint leaves (cools the spice)

**Steps** (Thin & Proper):

1. Boil/squeeze soya (8-10 min for softness).
2. Blitz ginger/chilies/mint with 100ml water into a paste.
3. Add soya + paste + lemon juice + jeera/salt to blender; pulse low 45 sec.
4. Top with remaining water—blend high 30 sec till frothy-thin (add more H2O if needed; lemon cuts thickness instantly).
5. Chill & sip—tastes like a spicy nimbu pani with protein!

**Why Swap?**: Zero dairy, super hydrating for Indian heat. Start with 2 lemons; ramp spice for that "wake-up" kick during late-night coding.

#### 2. **Tamarind-Chili Fireball Shake** (Deep Sour, Bold Spice – ~18g Protein, 130 Cal)

Tamarind (imli) gives a richer, fruity sour (like South Indian rasam) without creaminess—thins even better with its pulp water.

**Ingredients**:

- 60-70g dry soya chunks
- 2-3 tbsp tamarind pulp (soak golf-ball-sized imli in ½ cup hot water, strain—₹10 pack lasts weeks)
- 300ml water
- 1 tsp red chili powder (or 2 dry red chilies soaked) + ½ tsp hing
- 1 tsp coriander powder + pinch rock salt
- Optional: ½ tomato (blended for subtle body without thickness)

**Steps**:

1. Prep soya as usual.
2. Soak tamarind 5 min, mash & strain for sour liquid (discard seeds/fiber).
3. Blend soya + tamarind water + chili/hing/spices + 200ml water; low-speed first to avoid chunks.
4. Add tomato (if using) + rest of water; high-blend 30 sec—strains itself thin.
5. Room-temp or iced—fiery like a tangy chaat drink!

**Why Swap?**: Fermented tang aids digestion (better than dahi for some soy-sensitive guts), and it's vegan staple. Dial chili for "spicy enough to study" level.

#### Quick Tips for All:

- **Amp Spice/Sour**: Grate extra ginger for warmth, or add amchur powder (dry mango) for sour without liquid.
- **Thinness Lock**: Always 1:5 soya-to-liquid ratio here—pulse, don't over-blend (prevents foam-thickening).
- **Study Pro**: These are lighter than dahi versions—no heaviness, just zing to cut through screen fatigue. Batch-freeze pulp bases for instant shakes.
- **Test Run**: Lemon first—easiest. If sour's too sharp, dilute with cucumber juice.

#### Scaled-Up Sweet Soya Banana Shake (For 60-70g Soya – Post-Study or Pre-Workout)

Makes 1 large (600ml) or 2 medium shakes. Tastes like a creamy lassi—mild nutty banana vibe.

**Ingredients** (₹30-40 total):

- 60-70g dry soya chunks (~32-36g protein)
- 2 ripe bananas (mash one for extra smoothness)
- 400-500ml milk (or water + 2 tbsp curd for thinner vegan version)
- 10-12 almonds (soaked, for cream without thickness)
- 1-2 tsp honey/jaggery
- ½ tsp elaichi (cardamom) powder
- Optional: Handful spinach (blends invisible, adds micronutrients without bulk)

**Steps** (Proper Technique for Thin Consistency):

1. **Prep Soya Right**: Boil 60-70g chunks in 4-5 cups salted water for 8-10 min till super soft (test: fork-mashable). Drain, rinse cold water, then squeeze dry in a towel (wring like laundry—removes 90% water/fiber gunk that thickens).
2. **Rough Chop**: Cut chunks into small pieces—easier on blender.
3. **Blend in Layers**: First, banana + almonds + 200ml milk in blender; pulse 30 sec to puree (base liquid prevents clumping). Add soya pieces + remaining milk/water; blend 1 min on low, then 30 sec high. If thick, add 100ml more liquid gradually. (Pro: Add ice cubes for froth—thins + chills.)
4. **Strain & Chill**: Pour through sieve/cloth for silky texture (discard pulp if you want ultra-thin). Stir in honey/elaichi. Fridge 5 min or add ice.
5. **Study Mode**: Pour into a shaker bottle—sip while debugging code. Holds 4-6 hrs without separating.

**Why It Works for You**: Scaled protein hits your 50-60g daily goal in one go, but thinner version won't sit heavy. Banana's potassium fights soy's potential bloat.

#### Scaled-Up Savory Soya Masala Shake (For 60-70g Soya – Midday Study Fuel)

Lighter, tangy— like a spiced raita drink. Easier on stomach for all-day sessions.

**Ingredients** (₹25-35 total):

- 60-70g dry soya chunks
- 300g curd/dahi (thins naturally with tang)
- 300-400ml water/buttermilk (chaas base for flow)
- 1-inch ginger + 2 green chilies (grated—for zing without chew)
- 1 tsp jeera powder + pinch black salt/hing
- Handful mint/coriander (fresh, chopped—cools & dilutes)
- Optional: ½ cucumber (grated, watery thinner)

**Steps** (Thin & Proper):

1. **Boil & Squeeze**: Same as above—longer boil for mushy chunks.
2. **Flavor Blitz**: Grate ginger/chili/mint; mix with curd + jeera/salt in a bowl (pre-flavors soy).
3. **Blend Smart**: Chunks + curd mix + 200ml water first; blend 45 sec low-speed (avoids foam). Add cucumber/more water; pulse till pourable (like thin lassi). No straining needed—curd emulsifies.
4. **Adjust & Serve**: Taste-test (add lemon for extra thin/acid). Chill or room-temp sip.
5. **Study Hack**: Keep in thermos—refreshing for hot Indian afternoons, no brain fog.

**Ideas to Level Up**:

- **Batch for Week**: Double recipe, freeze in ice trays, thaw + blend as needed (thins on re-blend).
- **Thinner Twists**: Swap 20% soya for powdered soya (if available, ₹200/kg—blends instant). Or add oats (soak first) for sustained energy without glue.
- **Digestion Check**: 60-70g is ambitious—pair with ginger tea post-shake. If thick persists, your blender might need more power (₹1000 basic ones work fine).
- **Calorie Tweak**: For lean gains, add peanut butter (1 tbsp) for fats; skip for study-only.
